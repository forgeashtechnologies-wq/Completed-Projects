What is Root Canal Treatment?

Root canal treatment, also known as endodontic therapy, is a dental procedure designed to treat infection at the center of a tooth (the root canal system). The treatment involves removing the infected pulp, carefully cleaning and disinfecting the inside of the tooth, and then filling and sealing it.

When Do You Need Root Canal Treatment?

  • Severe toothache when chewing or applying pressure
  • Prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold temperatures
  • Discoloration or darkening of the tooth
  • Swelling and tenderness in nearby gums
  • Persistent or recurring pimple on the gums

Benefits of Root Canal Treatment

  • Eliminates infection and prevents its spread
  • Relieves tooth pain and sensitivity
  • Preserves your natural tooth
  • Restores normal biting force and sensation
  • Protects other teeth from excessive wear or strain

Our Root Canal Treatment Process

1. Diagnosis and Preparation

Our dentist will examine your tooth, take digital X-rays, and determine if root canal treatment is necessary. We'll explain the procedure and ensure you're comfortable before beginning.

2. The Procedure

Using local anesthesia, we'll ensure you feel no pain during the procedure. We'll create a small access opening in the tooth, remove the infected pulp, clean the canals, and shape them for filling.

3. Filling and Sealing

Once cleaned, the canals are filled with a biocompatible material called gutta-percha and sealed with adhesive cement. A temporary filling is placed to close the access opening.

4. Restoration

After the root canal treatment, we'll place a crown or other restoration on the tooth to protect it and restore full function. This final step is crucial for the long-term success of the treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is root canal treatment painful?

Modern root canal procedures at Tara's Dental are virtually painless thanks to effective local anesthesia and advanced techniques. Most patients report feeling comfortable throughout the procedure.

How many visits are required for root canal treatment?

Most root canal treatments at our Velachery clinic can be completed in 1-2 visits depending on the complexity of the case. Simple cases may be completed in a single sitting.

How long does a root canal treated tooth last?

With proper care and regular dental check-ups, a tooth that has had root canal treatment can last a lifetime. It's essential to protect the treated tooth with a crown or appropriate restoration.
